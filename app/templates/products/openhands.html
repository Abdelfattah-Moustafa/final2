How it works

OpenHands integrates as a virtual camera and microphone layer, compatible with any video conferencing platform — Zoom, Teams, Meet, and custom WebRTC endpoints. A lightweight client captures video feeds from participants, routes them through SignLens recognition on-device, and overlays live Arabic captions directly on the video tile.

In the reverse direction, whenever a hearing participant speaks, SignBridge's gloss engine renders a sign guide in the Deaf participant's overlay pane. The result is a seamless, fully bidirectional meeting experience where no participant needs a separate interface or interpreter.

Customer support

Technical architecture

OpenHands operates as a virtual device layer using the browser's getDisplayMedia and insertable streams APIs — no desktop application required. Each participant's video stream is processed through a WebWorker-based pipeline to avoid blocking the main thread, keeping the meeting UI responsive.

The caption and sign-guide overlays are rendered as a picture-in-picture canvas composited on top of the existing video tile. Enterprise deployments can connect OpenHands to their own on-premise inference servers via a simple REST + WebSocket API for air-gapped environments that cannot use cloud processing.
