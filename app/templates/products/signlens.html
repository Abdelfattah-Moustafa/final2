How it works

SignLens captures your hands through the camera and runs landmark detection using MediaPipe Holistic — 21 hand points, 33 body keypoints, all processed locally in real time. These landmarks feed a quantized TFLite classification model trained on Egyptian Sign Language vocabulary.

Recognized signs are streamed to the text area with rolling confidence windows. When a sign stabilizes above the detection threshold, it is committed to the transcript and, optionally, spoken aloud through the integrated TTS engine with a natural Arabic voice.

Technical architecture

SignLens runs a three-stage pipeline: landmark extraction via MediaPipe Holistic (WebGL backend), temporal smoothing with a 15-frame rolling window, and TFLite inference using the WebAssembly runtime. The model is a compact CNN-LSTM hybrid: a MobileNetV3-Small backbone extracts spatial features per frame, and a 2-layer LSTM models temporal dynamics.

The model weighs under 5 MB (int8 quantized) and loads in under 900ms on a mid-range device. No GPU required — CPU inference handles 30fps input streams comfortably. All computation is local: no video or landmark data ever leaves your device.
